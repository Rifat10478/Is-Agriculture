Agriculture FAQs

Crops like millet, sorghum, chickpeas, and certain varieties of beans thrive in dry climates due to their drought resistance and low water requirements.

Farmers can improve soil fertility by using organic compost, crop rotation, cover cropping, and incorporating green manure to enrich the soil with nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.

Sustainable farming includes practices like crop diversification, integrated pest management, water conservation, minimal tillage, and using renewable resources to maintain productivity without harming the environment.

Crop rotation helps prevent soil depletion, reduces pest and disease buildup, improves soil structure, and increases crop yields by alternating different types of crops in the same field.

Technology enables precision farming, efficient resource management, real-time monitoring, and automation, which increase productivity, reduce waste, and support sustainable agricultural practices.
